## The insight: a pricing GRID, not a pricing ladder

Two INDEPENDENT axes were discovered to compose into a pricing surface:

**Axis 1 — intelligence depth** (the tiers; see intelligence-layer design doc):
- Tier 1 accumulation (deterministic rates/trends)
- Tier 2 explainable pattern rules
- Tier 3 predictive ML (the AI roadmap)

**Axis 2 — organizational layer** (mapped early in planning):
- Operational (Dispatch) — line-of-business budget
- Executive (Director / Deputy GM Kandilige) — strategic budget; renews contracts
- Stewardship (union / contract / equity record) — institutional budget

Crossing them yields a grid where each cell is a real capability serving a
DIFFERENT buyer with a DIFFERENT budget line:
- Operational × T2 = "overflow before the work order" (Dispatch)
- Executive × T2 = "redistribution payoff / risk" (Director/Deputy)
- Executive/Stewardship × T3 = "capital forecast / predictive equity risk"
  (a government AI-for-asset-management innovation line item)
- Stewardship × T1 = "condition of record" (the EAMS-can't-do-this defensible
  record)

Why it deepens pockets: it's a land-and-expand surface. Land in operational,
expand to executive, renew on stewardship, upsell to predictive — the same
product expanding into multiple budgets within one org over time, rather than a
flat per-seat or per-stop fee.

## CRITICAL CAVEAT — do not weld access tiers into the pricing axis carelessly

The three DATA-ACCESS tiers (raw → normalized → MV) are a SEPARATE structure from
the intelligence tiers, and they exist for a TRUST reason: raw access was given
to analysts specifically so the BA team feels OWNERSHIP and can verify the
intelligence against raw counts — which converts them from skeptics to allies.

- Do NOT paywall raw data access. "Pay more to see your own (public) data" re-
  creates the exact distrust open access was meant to dissolve, and government
  buyers are especially prickly about being charged to access their own public
  data.
- Charge for the INTELLIGENCE built on top (depth × organizational breadth), not
  for the raw underneath. Raw access stays open as the trust-builder.
- Open question for the commercial stage: exactly where the paid line sits
  (probably: T1 substrate + raw access are table-stakes/open; T2 and T3
  intelligence and the executive/stewardship surfaces are the paid expansion).
